Chapter 11 - Direct & Indirect speech

Direct and Indirect Speech

Direct speech is quoted speech. Indirect speech is reported speech. In Pidgin as well as in regular English Direct speech is rendered with quotation marks when written.

Direct Speech

Examples of direct speech:

  • Pidgin: I love the line, “This is the end of Sid the sloth!”

  • Regular English: I dey love the line, “This is be the end of Sid the sloth!”

Indirect/Reported Speech

In regular English indirect speech sometimes uses the word “that” before making the actual report. Pidgin English does same, using the word “say” instead. For example:

  • He told me that it was time to start the show.

  • He tell me say den e be time to start the show.

Or without “that” (regular) and “say (pidgin):

  • He told me it was time to start the show

  • He tell me den e be time to start the show
