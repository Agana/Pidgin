Chapter 4 - Pronouns

Pronouns

Pronouns are among the most interesting words in Pidgin English. They are also used is some of its most interesting ways. Their basic grammar is the same. They replace or substitute for nouns in a sentence.

Types of Pronouns

Personal Pronouns

Features of Pronouns

Like nouns, pronouns have:

  • Gender
  • Number

Unlike nouns, however, they also have:

  • Person
  • Case

Gender

Pronouns take the gender of the nouns they replace. As a result, there are pronouns for each gender:

Feminine: she In pidgin the feminine pronoun is only used in the subject position (nominative case). For example:

  • She be waitress    (She is a waitress)

    Common: A/I (I), ma/my (my), E (he, she, who), she (she), een (his), am (een, her), dem (them, they, who), dema (their), wona (our), you (you), your (your), we (we, us), me (me)

Pidgin English shares most pronouns amongst all genders. All the above can be used for masculine, feminine and common gender nouns. For example:

  • I dey come    (I am coming)

In “let me…” statements, the first person “I or A” is used, instead of me. So:

  • Make a see your hand

    (Let me see your hand)

  • E be ma bro (He is my brother)

  • E be waitress (She is a waitress)

  • The man e come here yestee (The man who came here yesterday)

  • This be ma poppy een car (This is my father’s car)

  • Take give am (Give it to him/her)

  • Take give dem (Give it to them)

  • Dem take give am (They gave it to him/her)

  • The people dem come here (The people who came here)

  • This be dema house (This is their house)

  • This be wona time (This is our time)

  • You be tourist? (Are you a tourist?)

  • I go come visit you (I will come and visit you)

  • Make I feel your hand

(Let me feel your hand)

“We” in pidgin is use both for “we” and “us” in regular English usage:

  • We be the champions! (We are the champions!)

  • The soldiers sack we (The soldiers sacked us)

  • We wan make you take give we (We want you to give it to us)

Neuter: In addition, E, him/een, dem and dema can also be used for inanimate nouns:

  • E be spoon (It is a spoon)

  • The bowl een handles (The bowl’s handles)

  • Dem be very tall buildings (They are very tall buildings)

  • The doors dema handles (The handles of the doors)
