Chapter 10 - Interjections

Interjections

Interjections are used to express sudden emotion, such as shock, surprise (they are different), anger, hope or despair. They can be words or phrases, and often they are language specific. For example, “Oh my!” is quite universal in regular English, while “Bligh me!” is quite uniquely British.

Here are some common Pidgin English interjections:

Greeting and Address

  • Ma guy!

  • Chale!

  • Oga

  • Boss

  • Boosu

  • Director

  • Yo!

Pidgin English -- Regular English Equivalent

  • Ma guy! -- My man!
  • Chale! -- Friend, My man, Hey!
  • Beh you! -- But you!
  • Paa? -- It’s that serious?
  • E be so! -- That’s right! / - - Great! / Lovely!
  • I beg! -- Please! / Not now please!
  • Hey -- Hey!
  • Oh! -- Oh!
  • Dey g! -- Look out! / Be careful! / Be on guard!
  • Yaaaay! -- Huraaaay!
  • Ajei! -- Ouch!

  • Iyn* dat! -- That’s it!

  • Recall that in Pidgin him is pronounced like this.

  • When "Paa" is used in questions it is intoned like "why?"
